The four U.S. gastroenterology and hepatology societies have made history this year. For the first time, the presidents of AGA, AASLD, ACG and ASGE are women. 

To mark and celebrate this occasion, the four presidents have chronicled the rise of women in GI society leadership in a special article appearing online ahead of print in all four organizations’ professional journals: “Society Leadership and Diversity; Hail to the Women!” The article will be published in the September issue of Gastroenterology in honor of Women in Medicine Month.

The fact that women are in the top position at all four societies simultaneously is not by design, but by coincidence. This signals a strong recognition of the importance of diversity and inclusion in the profession. Women represent only 16.4 percent of the 14,107 actively practicing gastroenterologists in the U.S., but opportunities for women in society leadership positions have grown. 

The article looks at the history of women presidents in GI societies. All four have had remarkable women in the top position as long ago as 1942. But, they have been relatively few and far between. The gap has narrowed in recent years, with women rising to the president position more frequently. The piece also documents the percentage of each society’s overall leadership positions held by women. Each society has one or more programs or committees focused on creating mentorship, networking and learning opportunities for women and for all members with an eye toward diversity. 

In the article, the presidents give some valuable advice to other women in gastroenterology. And, they offer this call to action for the future: “Medical societies and their leaderships must stand for equality, remove barriers and continue to foster opportunities to build the cohort of emerging leaders in gastroenterology and hepatology.”
