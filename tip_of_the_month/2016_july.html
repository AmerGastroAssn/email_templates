TIP OF THE MONTH • July 2016  

Throughout your first year as an AGA member, look forward to monthly tips with essential information for making the most of your membership. Below is the tip for July 2016.

Are you looking to network with colleagues and be more active in AGA? AGA has many ways you can get involved and interact with other members to help establish association priorities and advance the science and practice of gastroenterology and hepatology.

Here are a few ways to get started:

  • AGA Community – join your peers in lively discussions that span the GI landscape and engage with colleagues who have similar professional interests or career paths via this members-only online forum. Grow your professional network year-round and start today by introducing yourself to the group.
  • AGA Institute Council and Sections — members can join up to six sections. The council addresses the diverse needs of its members and is made up of elected representatives from the 13 special interest groups, or sections, within AGA membership. Review each section’s page for ideas on how you can get involved.
  • Committee Appointments — the AGA committee structure enables volunteer members to play an active role in the association’s decision-making process. We are currently accepting nominations for committees and centers. You can nominate yourself or another AGA member. Nominations are due by Nov. 1, 2016.
  • Publish with AGA — submit your research electronically for publication in AGA journals: Gastroenterology , Clinical Gastroenterology and Hepatology and Cellular and Molecular Gastroenterology and Hepatology ). You can also submit your abstracts for consideration for presentation at Digestive Disease Week® (DDW).

Questions about your membership? Contact AGA Member Relations at 301-941-2651 or member@gastro.org.
